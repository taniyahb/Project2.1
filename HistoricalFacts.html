This is information about the History of
domestic abuse.
British law used to allow a man to "chastise" his wife with any thing. In the United States, in the 1800s, laws and practices supported a man's right to discipline his wife. It wasn't until 1895 that a woman could even divorce her husband on grounds of abuse.

In 1994, the Violence Against Women Act was adopted. This act generated legal and financial support for law enforcement and social services to protect battered women.
In the 1970s,Wife beating was defined as one extreme in a spectrum of male efforts to dominate women, and argued that rape was a crime of violence, not sex. Feminists founded shelters where women could take refuge and they demanded that the police do more to protect women
